Spanish

Description: These collaborative immersion courses focus on acquiring language proficiency through the use of authentic materials, including literature, music, film, discussions, and games. Students understand grammar in meaningful contexts and engage in project-based learning as they produce podcasts, make movies, and engage in small group projects. Students are assessed on their individual progress via video, audio, and written portfolios.

This Spanish class is very unique to me because it is immersive and challenges me to go out of my comfort zone every class. Participation is a large part of the course and I feel like I am learning more Spanish by being forced to use it. This class is also very fun with lots of group work, projects, and movies.

Diego Velazquez

This presentation of the artist Diego Velazquez was assigned to practice prepositions in Spanish to describe features of each work of art from the artist. Things which had to be said were:

— Date of Birth.

— Style.

— Origin.

— 3 different examples of artist’s work.

— Described colors.

— Described objects and people.

Spanish Memes

The Spanish Memes project practices formulating sentences in Spanish with a little fun added into it. All memes were put into a competition and winners were displayed on bulletin boards. Translated, these memes mean:

— First I will do math homework. Then I will do physics homework. Finally, I will do STEM homework. Finally, I will do STEM homework.

— Corporate needs you to find the differences between this picture and this picture. They are the same picture.

— Dr. C. correcting homework at half past one at night.

— The world if everyone speaks two languages.
