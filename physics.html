Physics

Description: This course takes an algebra and calculus-based approach to investigate and model connections between concepts, equations, and graphs. Through classroom discussions and collaborative work, students learn about mechanics, gravitation, electricity, magnetism, waves, and harmonic oscillations. They design, build, and debug their own experiments and lab apparatuses. They analyze their data using statistical methods and report their findings via journal-formatted printouts, poster boards, and slideshow presentations.

Although hard to conceptualize at first, physics at MAMS has been really engaging and exciting for me to learn. Everything unit builds of off the previous one and gives a physical meaning to the mathematical formulas learned in the math classes of years past. Predicting the movement of a projectile or the mass of an unknown object through indirect means has interested me in this class. Some of my labs are below.

Quantifying Motion

In this lab, I observed the motion of a battery operated car and a cart traveling down a low friction incline. I collected my data using a stop watch and tape measure to determine if the vehicle was moving at constant velocity or accelerating. If it was in motion or accelerating, I found the value at which it was moving or accelerating.

Surface Area vs. Friction

In this lab, I had to determine if the surface area of an object affects its coefficients of friction between a common surface. I did this by testing for both the static and kinetic coefficients of a tissue box (testing the different sides) on a piece of wood. Through my experimentation, I confirmed my hypothesis and concluded that surface area does not effect the coefficients of friction.
