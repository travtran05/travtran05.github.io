Humanities

Description: This course uses a variety of literary, historical, and cultural readings in combination with essay writing, group projects, and class discussions to answer the essential question: “What does it mean to be human?” Students will examine how the definitions and understandings of “humanity” have changed over time.

This humanities class is unlike any I have taken before. I have really enjoyed it because of the discussion based nature of the class and the ample time to work on essays. We spend most of the time discussing taboo topics in an open and respectful way and I really value that. Some of the pieces I have worked on both alone and with a group are below.

Thoreau Skit

The Thoreau Skit was a group play performed by myself, Anya Kelley, Joseph Yu, Mihika Chalasani, and Giang Pham for our all-school retreat to Camp Bournedale at the beginning of the school year to explore different interpretations of our summer reading book: Walden by Henry David Thoreau. Through the play, our group demonstrated that Thoreau wrote Walden to show that people use hypocrisy as a coping mechanism. The production and the performance of the play was a really fun process and was a great way to get to know my new classmates.

Why go to School?

This essay on education poses the question of whether or not school is needed in this day and age, as the internet gives everyone access to the vast library of human knowledge almost instantly. I argue that education is needed because it teaches students not only facts that are available on the internet, but teaches critical thinking and character as well.
